01. Overview & Learnings

I have been in touch with Professor Suresh Kumar since January 2025, and under his guidance spent the semester learning prerequisites to work on a project under him. This started with the basics of Semi-Riemannain Geometry, Einstein's Field Equations, the FLRW metric, the Friedmann Equations, etc. followed by relevant cosmological terms such as the Equation of State of the various fluids, and cosmological parameters, times and distances. I also learnt Bayesian analysis with considerable rigour.

I joined Professor Suresh formally as a research intern in the August of 2025, where I have learnt how to work with various relevant tools and frameworks such as PyMultiNest, MontePython, and the Cosmic Linear Anisotropy Solving System (CLASS). I built my own MCMC framework in the interest of deepening my understanding of methods in Bayesian Analysis.

I have also learnt how to work with various cosmological datasets such as Cosmic Chronometer data (CC Data), Baryonic Acoustic Oscialltion Data (BAO Data; DESI BAO data), the supernova datasets and the CMB data. This involved building an understanding of the physics relevant to these phenomenon. I developed a PyMultiNest framework for various cosmological models utilising multiple datasets.

Key Learnings (so far)

  • Aquired a grasp of Bayesian Analysis and tools that utlize it (such as MCMC and MultiNesting).
  • Developed a basic understanding of General Relativity along with the relevant mathematical background.
  • Solidified a strong foundation in basic cosmology.
  • Learnt how to work with cosmological tools and frameworks such as CLASS, MontePython and PyMultiNest.
  • Advanced my understanding of how cosmological data is collected and how to work with them.
